# Art direction

## Purpose
Art direction translates brand territory and campaign brief into a precise visual execution language. It is the single document Visual Designer and Motion Designer both follow to ensure consistency.

## Output structure

```
## Art direction — [Project name]

### Visual concept
[1-2 sentences describing the core visual idea. What does this campaign *look* like? What is the dominant visual metaphor or aesthetic?]

---

### Photography / imagery direction
**Subject**: [what appears in images — people, objects, environments, abstractions]
**Treatment**: [editing style — raw/clean/textured/minimal/bold]
**Composition**: [tight/wide/centered/dynamic/rule-of-thirds]
**Mood**: [adjectives that describe the feeling of the imagery]
**Reference**: [describe 2-3 visual references — not brand names, but visual descriptions]
**Avoid**: [what should never appear]

---

### Graphic language
**Shapes**: [geometric/organic/angular/curved — and why]
**Lines**: [weight, style, usage]
**Texture**: [present/absent — if present, describe]
**Space**: [dense/airy/structured/free]
**Motion principles**: [how elements enter, exit, and behave — feeds into Motion Designer]

---

### Typography in use
**Display**: [typeface or style + weight + size range + usage context]
**Body**: [typeface or style + weight + usage context]
**Special treatments**: [any editorial typographic moments — oversized, outlined, layered]

---

### Color in execution
**Primary application**: [how the primary brand color is used — backgrounds, text, accents]
**Secondary application**: [same for secondary]
**Contrast ratios**: [confirm WCAG AA compliance]
**Dark vs. light surfaces**: [when to use each]

---

### Format-specific notes
**Feed posts**: [specific art direction for static feed]
**Stories/reels**: [specific art direction for vertical video]
**Video**: [specific art direction for 16:9 or other formats]
**OOH/print** (if applicable): [specific notes]

---

### What this is NOT
- Not: [specific visual style to avoid]
- Not: [specific treatment to avoid]
- Not: [common mistake for this brand/campaign]
```

## Handoff instructions
After posting the art direction:
- @mention Visual Designer: "Art direction ready. Please align your asset specs with this direction."
- @mention Motion Designer: "Art direction ready. Graphic language section defines motion principles — please align your motion brief."
- @mention Creative Director: "Art direction posted for review."

## Rules
- Every decision must connect back to the brand territory from Brand Strategist.
- "Reference" means visual description only — never paste brand names or copyrighted work.
- If a format is not in the campaign brief, skip that section entirely.
- Art direction is not a mood board — it must be actionable. Every designer should be able to produce assets from this document without asking follow-up questions.
